# Social Media Strategy

Comprehensive reference for building and executing social media strategies across all major platforms. Covers platform-specific tactics, content planning, growth frameworks, competitor analysis, trend monitoring, influencer collaboration, and crisis management.

## 1. Platform-Specific Strategies

### 1.1 LinkedIn

**Audience**: B2B professionals, decision-makers, job seekers, industry thought leaders.

**Algorithm Priorities**:
- Dwell time (how long users spend reading your post)
- Early engagement within the first 60-90 minutes
- Comments weighted more than reactions
- Native content preferred over external links
- Document/carousel posts get extended reach

**Content Types (by effectiveness)**:
1. Document/carousel posts (highest reach)
2. Text-only posts with line breaks and hooks
3. Native video (under 3 minutes)
4. Polls (high engagement, lower quality signals)
5. Articles (long-form, lower reach but high authority)
6. External link posts (lowest organic reach)

**Posting Cadence**: 3-5 posts per week (weekdays, 7-9 AM or 12-1 PM local time of target audience).

**LinkedIn Content Template**:
```
[Hook line that stops the scroll — bold claim, question, or surprising stat]

[Line break]

[2-3 sentences expanding on the hook with personal experience or data]

[Line break]

[3-5 bullet points with actionable takeaways, each starting with an emoji or symbol]

[Line break]

[Call to action — question to prompt comments]

[Line break]

[3-5 relevant hashtags]
```

**LinkedIn Growth Tactics**:
- Comment thoughtfully on 10-15 posts from target audience daily before posting
- Use the "POV:" format for thought leadership (e.g., "POV: You're a CTO who just discovered...")
- Share behind-the-scenes company stories with real numbers
- Tag relevant people (sparingly, only when genuinely relevant)
- Repurpose long blog posts as carousel documents (PDF uploads)
- Engage in comments for at least 30 minutes after posting
- Join and participate in relevant LinkedIn groups
- Use LinkedIn newsletters for recurring content (subscribers get notified)

**LinkedIn Profile Optimization Checklist**:
```
[ ] Headline uses keywords + value proposition (not just job title)
[ ] Banner image reflects brand or current campaign
[ ] About section tells a story with keywords in first 2 lines
[ ] Featured section showcases top 3 pieces of content
[ ] Experience section reads like achievements, not responsibilities
[ ] Custom URL claimed (linkedin.com/in/yourname)
[ ] Creator mode enabled with relevant topics
[ ] Recommendations from clients/peers (aim for 5+)
```

### 1.2 X / Twitter

**Audience**: Real-time conversations, tech, media, politics, crypto, creators.

**Algorithm Priorities**:
- Recency and velocity of engagement
- Replies and quote tweets weighted heavily
- Bookmark signals (save for later = high quality signal)
- Profile authority (follower ratio, account age, verification)
- Topic clustering (algorithm groups you by what you tweet about)

**Content Types (by effectiveness)**:
1. Threads (5-15 tweets, educational or storytelling)
2. Text tweets with strong hooks
3. Image tweets (memes, infographics, screenshots)
4. Short video (under 60 seconds)
5. Polls (engagement bait but useful for research)
6. Quote tweets with added insight

**Posting Cadence**: 3-7 tweets per day (including replies). Peak times: 8-10 AM, 12-1 PM, 5-6 PM EST.

**Thread Template**:
```
Tweet 1 (Hook):
[Bold claim or promise] — A thread:

Tweet 2 (Context):
[Why this matters / background]

Tweets 3-8 (Value):
[One point per tweet, numbered]
[Use visuals where possible]

Tweet 9 (Summary):
TL;DR:
- Point 1
- Point 2
- Point 3

Tweet 10 (CTA):
[Ask for retweet, follow, or reply]
[Link to relevant resource if applicable]
```

**X/Twitter Growth Tactics**:
- Quote tweet viral posts with genuine insight (not just "Great post!")
- Reply to large accounts within 5 minutes of their tweets
- Use "bookmark bait" (content people want to save: checklists, frameworks, templates)
- Create recurring series (e.g., "Tool Tuesday", "Friday Frameworks")
- Engage in Twitter Spaces as a speaker or host
- Pin your highest-performing or most representative tweet
- Use Twitter Lists to monitor competitors, industry leaders, and prospects
- Leverage trending topics when genuinely relevant (newsjacking)

### 1.3 Instagram

**Audience**: Visual-first consumers, lifestyle, fashion, food, travel, B2C brands.

**Algorithm Priorities**:
- Saves and shares weighted highest
- Reels get 2-3x the reach of static posts
- Story engagement (polls, questions, sliders) boosts feed visibility
- Consistency signals (regular posting schedule)
- Relationship signals (DMs, comment replies, story reactions)

**Content Types (by effectiveness)**:
1. Reels (15-60 seconds, trending audio, educational or entertaining)
2. Carousel posts (educational, swipeable, save-worthy)
3. Stories (daily, behind-the-scenes, interactive stickers)
4. Static image posts (quotes, announcements, product shots)
5. Live video (real-time engagement, Q&A sessions)
6. Guides (curated collections of posts)

**Posting Cadence**: 4-7 Reels per week, 2-3 carousels per week, daily Stories. Peak times: 11 AM-1 PM and 7-9 PM local time.

**Reels Best Practices**:
```
Structure:
1. Hook (first 1-3 seconds) — text overlay + movement
2. Value delivery (10-30 seconds) — teach, show, or entertain
3. CTA (last 3-5 seconds) — follow, save, share, comment

Technical:
- Vertical format (9:16, 1080x1920)
- Use trending audio (check Reels tab for trending sounds)
- Add captions/subtitles (85% watch without sound)
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags in caption
- Share Reel to Stories for extra reach
- Post at consistent times
```

**Carousel Template**:
```
Slide 1: Bold headline + hook (make them swipe)
Slide 2: Problem statement or "Why this matters"
Slides 3-8: One tip/point per slide with visual consistency
Slide 9: Summary or recap
Slide 10: CTA — "Save this for later" / "Share with someone who needs this"
```

### 1.4 YouTube

**Audience**: Long-form learners, how-to seekers, entertainment consumers.

**Algorithm Priorities**:
- Watch time and average view duration (most important)
- Click-through rate on thumbnails (CTR target: 5-10%)
- Session time (does your video lead viewers to watch more YouTube?)
- Engagement velocity in first 24-48 hours
- Subscriber conversion rate

**Content Types (by effectiveness)**:
1. Long-form tutorials/educational (10-20 minutes)
2. YouTube Shorts (under 60 seconds, vertical)
3. Listicles and compilations
4. Behind-the-scenes and vlogs
5. Interviews and collaborations
6. Live streams

**Publishing Cadence**: 1-2 long-form videos per week, 3-5 Shorts per week. Consistency matters more than frequency.

**Video Structure Template**:
```
0:00-0:30  — Hook: Preview the outcome or ask a compelling question
0:30-1:30  — Intro: Quick context, credibility, what they'll learn
1:30-X:00  — Main content: Deliver value in logical sections
             Use pattern interrupts every 2-3 minutes (B-roll, graphics, angle changes)
X:00-end   — CTA: Subscribe, watch next video (link in end screen), comment prompt

Title Formula: [Number] + [Outcome] + [Timeframe/Method]
Examples:
  - "7 Marketing Strategies That Generated $1M in 2026"
  - "How I Grew 10K Followers in 30 Days (Step-by-Step)"
  - "Stop Making These 5 LinkedIn Mistakes"
```

**Thumbnail Best Practices**:
```
Design Rules:
- 1280x720 pixels minimum (16:9 ratio)
- Large text (readable on mobile) — max 5-6 words
- High contrast colors (yellow, red, blue stand out)
- Expressive face showing emotion (if personal brand)
- Before/after or transformation visuals
- Consistent brand template across videos
- Avoid clutter — one focal point

A/B Test: Use YouTube's built-in thumbnail testing feature
```

### 1.5 Facebook

**Audience**: Broad demographics (skewing 30-65+), local communities, groups.

**Algorithm Priorities**:
- Meaningful interactions (comments, shares to Messenger)
- Group activity weighted heavily
- Video (especially live) gets priority
- Content from friends/family prioritized over pages
- Engagement bait penalized (e.g., "Tag a friend who...")

**Content Strategy for Pages**:
- Focus on Facebook Groups (organic reach for pages is 2-5%)
- Use Facebook Live for events, Q&A, announcements
- Create video content (native upload, not YouTube links)
- Leverage Facebook Events for community building
- Cross-post Reels from Instagram
- Run engagement-focused ads to supplement organic reach

**Facebook Groups Strategy**:
```
Setup:
1. Create a branded community group (not a fan page)
2. Set clear group rules (pinned post)
3. Create welcome post template for new members
4. Schedule weekly themed discussion threads

Growth:
- Invite email list subscribers and existing customers
- Promote group in all other channels
- Run "invite a friend" campaigns
- Cross-promote with complementary groups
- Go live weekly in the group

Engagement:
- Post daily discussion prompts
- Celebrate member milestones
- Run polls and surveys
- Share exclusive content only available in the group
- Spotlight member success stories
```

### 1.6 TikTok

**Audience**: Gen Z and millennials, entertainment-first, trend-driven.

**Algorithm Priorities**:
- Watch-through rate (most critical — percentage who watch to the end)
- Replays and loop completion
- Shares (to friends via DM)
- Comments and duets
- Content relevance to niche (topic consistency)
- Does NOT heavily weight follower count (small accounts can go viral)

**Content Strategy**:
```
Content Pillars for TikTok:
1. Educational (teach something in 30-60 seconds)
2. Behind-the-scenes (humanize the brand)
3. Trend participation (use trending sounds/formats with brand twist)
4. User-generated content (duets, stitches, challenges)
5. Storytelling (mini-narratives, day-in-the-life)

Video Structure:
- Hook: First 1-2 seconds must stop the scroll
- Body: Deliver value quickly (no filler)
- Loop: End feeds back into the beginning (for replays)
- CTA: Text overlay or verbal prompt

Technical:
- 9:16 vertical format
- 15-60 seconds optimal (under 30 for max completion rate)
- Use trending sounds (check Creative Center)
- Add text overlays (many watch without sound)
- Post 1-3 times daily for growth phase
- Use TikTok's built-in editing tools (algorithm may favor native editing)
```

### 2.2 Content Production Pipeline

```
STAGE 1: IDEATION (Week -2)
├── Review analytics for top-performing content
├── Check trending topics and industry news
├── Survey audience (polls, DMs, comments)
├── Brainstorm session: 20 ideas → filter to 10
└── Map ideas to content pillars and platforms

STAGE 2: CREATION (Week -1)
├── Write copy drafts for all posts
├── Design visuals (Canva/Figma templates)
├── Record and edit video content
├── Create carousel/document assets
├── Write alt text for accessibility
└── Internal review and approval

STAGE 3: SCHEDULING (Week -1, Friday)
├── Upload to scheduling tool (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later)
├── Set optimal posting times per platform
├── Queue Stories content
├── Prepare engagement prompts
└── Brief community manager on key messages

STAGE 4: PUBLISHING & ENGAGEMENT (Live week)
├── Monitor first 60 minutes of each post
├── Respond to all comments within 2 hours
├── Engage with audience content (like, comment, share)
├── Cross-promote top-performing posts
└── Document real-time insights

STAGE 5: ANALYSIS (Week +1)
├── Pull performance metrics per post
├── Compare against benchmarks
├── Identify top 3 and bottom 3 performers
├── Extract learnings for next cycle
└── Update content strategy based on data
```

## 3. Content Pillar Planning

### 3.1 The Four Pillar Framework

```
PILLAR 1: EDUCATIONAL (40% of content)
Purpose: Establish expertise and provide value
Formats: How-to guides, tutorials, tips, frameworks, checklists
Examples:
  - "5 Ways to Improve Your Email Open Rate"
  - Step-by-step tutorial carousel
  - Quick tip Reels/Shorts
  - Industry trend analysis threads
KPIs: Saves, shares, website clicks

PILLAR 2: PROMOTIONAL (20% of content)
Purpose: Drive business results (leads, sales, signups)
Formats: Product features, case studies, testimonials, launches, offers
Examples:
  - Customer success story with metrics
  - Product demo video
  - Limited-time offer announcement
  - Webinar/event promotion
KPIs: Clicks, conversions, leads generated

PILLAR 3: ENGAGEMENT (25% of content)
Purpose: Build community and spark conversation
Formats: Polls, questions, debates, user-generated content, memes
Examples:
  - "What's your biggest challenge with [topic]?"
  - This-or-that polls
  - Caption contests
  - "Unpopular opinion: [take]"
  - Fill-in-the-blank prompts
KPIs: Comments, shares, DMs, poll votes

PILLAR 4: CULTURE / BRAND (15% of content)
Purpose: Humanize the brand and build emotional connection
Formats: Behind-the-scenes, team spotlights, values, celebrations, stories
Examples:
  - Team member introduction
  - Office/workspace tour
  - Company milestone celebration
  - Day-in-the-life content
  - Holiday and awareness day participation
KPIs: Follower growth, sentiment, brand mentions
```

## 4. Growth Tactics by Platform

### 4.1 Universal Growth Playbook

```
Phase 1: Foundation (0-1K followers) — Months 1-3
├── Optimize profile/bio on all platforms
├── Post consistently (minimum 3x/week per platform)
├── Engage with 20-30 accounts daily in your niche
├── Join relevant communities and groups
├── Cross-promote between platforms
└── Focus on 1-2 primary platforms, maintain presence on others

Phase 2: Momentum (1K-10K) — Months 3-6
├── Collaborate with peers at similar follower counts
├── Participate in trending topics (newsjacking)
├── Launch a recurring content series
├── Start building an email list from social followers
├── Experiment with paid promotion on top-performing posts
└── Analyze data weekly and double down on what works

Phase 3: Scale (10K-100K) — Months 6-18
├── Partner with influencers and complementary brands
├── Launch user-generated content campaigns
├── Invest in video content production
├── Build community (Discord, Facebook Group, etc.)
├── Diversify to additional platforms
├── Develop brand voice guidelines for team members
└── Implement social listening tools

Phase 4: Authority (100K+) — Ongoing
├── Become a go-to source for industry commentary
├── Host events (virtual and in-person)
├── Launch products/services to monetize audience
├── Build media relationships for amplification
├── Mentor smaller creators (builds loyalty and reach)
└── Focus on retention and community depth, not just growth
```

### 4.2 Platform-Specific Growth Accelerators

```
LinkedIn:
- "Creator Mode" activation with 5 relevant hashtag topics
- Weekly LinkedIn newsletter (subscribers get push notifications)
- Engagement pods (coordinate with peers to boost early engagement)
- Employee advocacy (team members share and comment)
- LinkedIn Live events for real-time interaction

X/Twitter:
- Strategic quote tweeting of viral content with unique insight
- Twitter Spaces hosting on niche topics
- Pinned thread that showcases your best work
- Engagement with replies on large accounts' tweets
- "Mutuals" strategy — build relationships with similar creators

Instagram:
- Collaboration posts (shared audience reach)
- Reels using trending audio within 48 hours of trend start
- Story interactive features daily (polls, questions, quizzes)
- Save-optimized carousel content
- Hashtag strategy: 3-5 niche hashtags (not broad ones)

YouTube:
- Optimize for search (keyword-rich titles and descriptions)
- Create playlist-based content series
- End screen cards linking to related videos
- Community tab polls and updates
- Shorts to drive subscribers to long-form content

TikTok:
- Post 2-3x daily during growth phase
- Stitch and duet popular creators in your niche
- Participate in trends within first 24-48 hours
- Use TikTok Creative Center for trending sounds
- Engage heavily in comments on your own videos
```

## 6. Trend Monitoring and Newsjacking

### 6.1 Trend Monitoring System

```
REAL-TIME SOURCES:
├── X/Twitter Trending Topics (check 3x daily)
├── Google Trends (daily check for industry terms)
├── TikTok Creative Center (trending sounds and hashtags)
├── Instagram Explore page (visual trend identification)
├── Reddit front page and relevant subreddits
├── Industry news RSS feeds
└── Social listening tools (Brandwatch, Sprout Social, Mention)

EVALUATION FRAMEWORK (before jumping on a trend):
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ TREND EVALUATION CHECKLIST                       │
│                                                  │
│ [ ] Relevance: Does this connect to our brand?  │
│ [ ] Timeliness: Can we create content in <4 hrs?│
│ [ ] Audience: Will our audience care?            │
│ [ ] Risk: Could this backfire? (check sentiment) │
│ [ ] Value: Can we add unique insight?            │
│ [ ] Alignment: Does it fit our brand voice?      │
│                                                  │
│ Score: [X/6] — Proceed if 4+ checks pass        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

RESPONSE SPEED TARGETS:
- Breaking industry news: < 2 hours
- Viral meme/format: < 4 hours
- Trending topic: < 8 hours
- Cultural moment: < 24 hours
- Seasonal/planned event: Pre-scheduled
```

### 6.2 Newsjacking Template

```
FORMAT: [Industry News] + [Your Unique Angle]

Step 1: Identify the news/trend
Step 2: Determine your brand's relevant perspective
Step 3: Create content that adds value (not just commentary)
Step 4: Publish quickly with relevant hashtags
Step 5: Engage in the conversation (replies, quote tweets)

Example:
News: "Google announces major algorithm update"
Brand angle (marketing agency): "Here's what this means for your SEO strategy"
Content: Quick thread/carousel with 5 actionable steps to adapt
```

## 7. Influencer Collaboration Patterns

### 7.1 Influencer Tier Framework

```
TIER 1: NANO (1K-10K followers)
├── Cost: $50-$250 per post or product exchange
├── Engagement rate: 4-8% (highest)
├── Best for: Local businesses, niche products, authentic reviews
├── Approach: Direct DM, personal relationship
└── Volume strategy: Work with 20-50 nano influencers

TIER 2: MICRO (10K-100K followers)
├── Cost: $250-$2,500 per post
├── Engagement rate: 2-5%
├── Best for: Targeted campaigns, niche authority
├── Approach: Email with clear brief, negotiate package deals
└── Volume strategy: Work with 5-15 micro influencers

TIER 3: MID-TIER (100K-500K followers)
├── Cost: $2,500-$15,000 per post
├── Engagement rate: 1-3%
├── Best for: Brand awareness campaigns, product launches
├── Approach: Through management or direct pitch
└── Volume strategy: Work with 2-5 mid-tier influencers

TIER 4: MACRO (500K-1M followers)
├── Cost: $15,000-$50,000 per post
├── Engagement rate: 1-2%
├── Best for: Mass awareness, brand credibility
├── Approach: Through talent agencies
└── Volume strategy: 1-2 per campaign

TIER 5: MEGA (1M+ followers)
├── Cost: $50,000+ per post
├── Engagement rate: 0.5-1.5%
├── Best for: Major launches, brand repositioning
├── Approach: Through agencies with existing relationships
└── Volume strategy: 1 per campaign (if budget allows)
```

## 8. Crisis Management on Social Media

### 8.1 Crisis Severity Levels

```
LEVEL 1 — LOW (Negative comment/review)
├── Response time: < 4 hours
├── Handler: Community manager
├── Action: Respond professionally, offer resolution
├── Escalation: None unless pattern emerges
└── Documentation: Log in tracking sheet

LEVEL 2 — MEDIUM (Viral negative post, minor controversy)
├── Response time: < 2 hours
├── Handler: Social media manager + PR lead
├── Action: Acknowledge, investigate, provide update
├── Escalation: Notify marketing director
└── Documentation: Incident report, response log

LEVEL 3 — HIGH (Brand reputation threat, boycott, data breach, executive scandal)
├── Response time: < 1 hour
├── Handler: Crisis team (CEO, PR, Legal, Social)
├── Action: Pause all scheduled content, draft holding statement
├── Escalation: Full crisis team activation
└── Documentation: Full incident report, media monitoring
```

### 8.2 Crisis Response Playbook

```
STEP 1: ASSESS (0-30 minutes)
├── What happened? (Facts only, no assumptions)
├── Where is it spreading? (Which platforms, hashtags)
├── What is the sentiment? (Ratio of negative to neutral/positive)
├── Is it escalating or stabilizing?
├── Who is involved? (Customers, media, employees, competitors)
└── PAUSE all scheduled content immediately

STEP 2: ASSEMBLE (30-60 minutes)
├── Notify crisis team (pre-defined contact list)
├── Assign roles: Spokesperson, Monitor, Responder, Legal review
├── Gather all facts from internal teams
├── Review legal implications
└── Draft initial response (see templates below)

STEP 3: RESPOND (1-2 hours)
├── Post holding statement on affected platforms
├── Respond to direct inquiries with consistent messaging
├── Do NOT delete negative posts (screenshots exist forever)
├── Do NOT argue or get defensive
├── Monitor response to your statement
└── Prepare FAQ for common questions

STEP 4: MANAGE (ongoing)
├── Update community regularly (every 2-4 hours for Level 3)
├── Monitor sentiment shifts
├── Address misinformation with facts
├── Engage media if they're covering the story
├── Document all actions and decisions
└── Continue pausing promotional content

STEP 5: RECOVER (post-crisis)
├── Issue final resolution statement
├── Implement changes to prevent recurrence
├── Thank supporters and community
├── Gradually resume normal content (wait at least 24-48 hours)
├── Conduct post-mortem analysis
└── Update crisis playbook with learnings
```

### 8.3 Crisis Response Templates

```
HOLDING STATEMENT (use while gathering facts):
"We're aware of [situation] and are looking into it. We take this
seriously and will share an update as soon as we have more information.
Thank you for your patience."

ACKNOWLEDGMENT (once facts are confirmed):
"We want to address [situation]. Here's what we know: [facts].
We understand this is concerning and we're [specific action being taken].
We'll continue to provide updates as we work through this."

APOLOGY (when the brand is at fault):
"We made a mistake and we're sorry. [What happened]. This doesn't
reflect our values. Here's what we're doing to fix it: [specific
actions]. We'll do better."

CORRECTION (when misinformation is spreading):
"We've seen [incorrect claim] circulating. Here are the facts:
[accurate information with evidence]. We're happy to answer any
questions."

RESOLUTION (closing the crisis):
"Update on [situation]: [What was done]. We've [permanent changes
made]. Thank you to our community for holding us accountable.
If you have additional concerns, please reach out to [contact]."
```

### 8.4 Crisis Prevention Checklist

```

[ ] Social listening tool monitoring brand mentions 24/7
[ ] Crisis team identified with contact information
[ ] Crisis playbook documented and accessible
[ ] Spokesperson media trained
[ ] Brand voice guidelines documented for sensitive topics
[ ] List of topics/stances the brand will NOT engage on
[ ] Pre-approved holding statements ready
[ ] Relationship with PR/legal for quick review
[ ] Regular crisis simulation exercises (quarterly)
[ ] Employee social media policy in place
[ ] Approval workflow for sensitive content
[ ] Escalation path clearly defined
```
