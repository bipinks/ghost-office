# Copywriting Patterns — Frameworks, Formulas & Conversion Copy

## 1. Core Copywriting Frameworks

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

The classic direct-response framework for structured persuasion:

```
AIDA FRAMEWORK
================
A — ATTENTION:  Grab the reader with a bold headline or opening statement.
                Stop the scroll. Interrupt the pattern. Address the pain.

I — INTEREST:   Build curiosity with relevant facts, stories, or data.
                Show you understand their situation. Introduce the topic.

D — DESIRE:     Make them want the solution. Paint the outcome.
                Use benefits (not features), social proof, and specificity.

A — ACTION:     Tell them exactly what to do next. One clear CTA.
                Remove friction. Create urgency if genuine.
```

**AIDA Example — Landing Page Hero Section:**

```
[ATTENTION]
Stop losing deals because your quotes take 3 days to send.

[INTEREST]
Sales teams using manual quoting processes spend 40% of their time
on admin instead of selling. Your competitors are sending quotes
in minutes, not days.

[DESIRE]
With automated quoting, your team creates professional, accurate
quotes in under 5 minutes. Customers get proposals while they are
still interested. Your pipeline moves faster. Revenue grows.

[ACTION]
Start your free trial — no credit card required.
[CTA Button: "Create Your First Quote Free"]
```

### PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

Best for audiences who are problem-aware but not solution-aware:

```
PAS FRAMEWORK
===============
P — PROBLEM:    State the problem clearly. Name it.
                Use the reader's own language.

A — AGITATE:    Make the pain vivid. Show consequences.
                What happens if they do nothing?

S — SOLUTION:   Present your solution as the relief.
                Connect the solution directly to the pain points you raised.
```

**PAS Example — Email Body:**

```
[PROBLEM]
Your inventory counts never match your system.

[AGITATE]
Every month-end, your team spends two full days reconciling stock.
Customers order items you thought were in stock but are not.
You write off thousands in shrinkage you cannot explain.
And the longer it goes unaddressed, the worse the discrepancies get.

[SOLUTION]
Our real-time inventory tracking eliminates the guesswork.
Every movement is logged automatically — receipts, transfers,
adjustments, and sales. Your system matches your shelves.
Month-end reconciliation takes minutes, not days.

See it in action: [Book a 15-minute demo]
```

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge

Best for painting a transformation story:

```
BAB FRAMEWORK
===============
B — BEFORE:     Describe the current painful state.
A — AFTER:      Describe the ideal future state.
B — BRIDGE:     Show how your product/service is the bridge between them.
```

**BAB Example — Case Study Introduction:**

```
[BEFORE]
Acme Manufacturing ran payroll manually every month. It took their
HR team three full days, produced frequent errors, and generated
a steady stream of employee complaints.

[AFTER]
Today, payroll runs automatically on the 25th. Processing takes
20 minutes. Errors have dropped to near zero. The HR team
spends their time on strategic work instead of spreadsheets.

[BRIDGE]
The difference? They replaced their manual process with automated
payroll that handles calculations, deductions, and statutory
compliance in a single run. Here is how they made the switch.
```

### 4Ps — Promise, Picture, Proof, Push

Best for sales pages and product marketing:

```
4Ps FRAMEWORK
===============
Promise:    Make a specific, compelling promise (the big benefit)
Picture:    Help the reader visualize the outcome
Proof:      Back it up with evidence (data, testimonials, case studies)
Push:       Give a reason to act now and a clear CTA
```

**4Ps Example — Feature Page:**

```
[PROMISE]
Generate professional invoices in 60 seconds.

[PICTURE]
Imagine this: a customer confirms an order. You click "Create Invoice,"
select the sales order, and a formatted PDF is ready to send — with your
logo, payment terms, tax calculations, and line items all populated
automatically. No retyping. No spreadsheets. No errors.

[PROOF]
"We used to spend 20 minutes per invoice. Now it is under a minute.
We invoice the same day instead of waiting until Friday."
— Sarah Chen, Operations Manager at Meridian Supply

Companies using automated invoicing collect payment 11 days faster
on average (source: Ardent Partners, 2025).

[PUSH]
Start invoicing faster today. Free for your first 50 invoices.
[CTA: "Start Free"]
```

### PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Transformation, Offer, Response

Extended framework for long-form sales copy:

```
PASTOR FRAMEWORK
==================
P — Problem:         Identify and name the core problem
A — Amplify:         Show the cost of not solving it
S — Story/Solution:  Tell a relatable story, then introduce the solution
T — Transformation:  Show the before-and-after change
O — Offer:           Present what they get (product, features, bonuses)
R — Response:        Ask for the specific action with urgency
```

## 2. Headline Formulas

### Proven Headline Patterns

```
HEADLINE FORMULAS
==================

HOW-TO:
  "How to [achieve desired outcome] without [common objection]"
  "How to [achieve desired outcome] in [specific timeframe]"
  Example: "How to close your books in 2 hours instead of 2 days"

NUMBERED LIST:
  "[Number] ways to [achieve outcome]"
  "[Number] [noun] that [verb] [outcome]"
  Example: "7 inventory mistakes that silently kill your margins"

QUESTION:
  "Are you still [painful activity]?"
  "What if you could [desirable outcome]?"
  Example: "Are you still reconciling inventory by hand?"

DIRECT BENEFIT:
  "[Achieve outcome] with [product/method]"
  "The fastest way to [desired outcome]"
  Example: "The fastest way to generate multi-branch financial reports"

SOCIAL PROOF:
  "How [customer] [achieved result] in [timeframe]"
  "Why [number] [type of customer] switched to [product]"
  Example: "How Meridian Supply cut invoicing time by 90%"

NEGATIVE/WARNING:
  "Stop [common mistake] before it [consequence]"
  "[Number] [category] mistakes to avoid in [year]"
  Example: "Stop approving purchase orders without three-way matching"

CURIOSITY GAP:
  "The [adjective] [topic] strategy nobody talks about"
  "We analyzed [number] [things]. Here is what we found."
  Example: "We analyzed 10,000 invoices. Here is why 23% were paid late."

SPECIFIC RESULT:
  "[Exact result] in [exact timeframe] — here is how"
  Example: "94% on-time delivery in 90 days — here is how"
```

### Headline Testing Guidelines

```
HEADLINE A/B TESTING
=====================
Always test:
- Length: Short (5-7 words) vs. medium (8-12 words) vs. long (13+)
- Format: Question vs. statement vs. how-to vs. number
- Specificity: Generic outcome vs. specific metric
- Emotion: Rational benefit vs. emotional trigger

Rules:
- Test one variable at a time
- Need minimum 100 observations per variant for significance
- Run for at least 7 days to account for day-of-week effects
- Winning headline must beat control by at least 10% to be meaningful
```

## 3. CTA Optimization

### CTA Copywriting Rules

```
CTA BEST PRACTICES
====================

1. START WITH A VERB
   Bad:  "Free trial"
   Good: "Start your free trial"

2. STATE THE BENEFIT, NOT THE ACTION
   Bad:  "Submit"
   Good: "Get my report"

3. BE SPECIFIC
   Bad:  "Learn more"
   Good: "See pricing plans"

4. REDUCE FRICTION
   Bad:  "Sign up for a demo"
   Good: "See it in action — no signup needed"

5. CREATE LEGITIMATE URGENCY (only when real)
   Bad:  "Buy now!!!" (fake urgency)
   Good: "Start free — upgrade anytime" (low commitment)
   Good: "Offer ends March 31" (real deadline)

6. MATCH CTA TO FUNNEL STAGE
   Awareness:    "Read the guide" / "Watch the video"
   Consideration: "Compare plans" / "See how it works"
   Decision:      "Start free trial" / "Book a demo"
   Retention:     "Upgrade your plan" / "Explore new features"
```

### CTA Placement Patterns

```
LANDING PAGE CTA PLACEMENT
============================
1. Hero section:       Primary CTA above the fold
2. After benefits:     Reinforce CTA after value proposition
3. After social proof: CTA near testimonials/case studies
4. Bottom of page:     Final CTA for scrollers

BLOG POST CTA PLACEMENT
=========================
1. After introduction: Contextual CTA related to the topic
2. Mid-article:        Inline CTA (text link or banner)
3. End of article:     Primary CTA matching the content's intent

EMAIL CTA PLACEMENT
=====================
1. Primary CTA:        Above the fold (visible without scrolling)
2. Secondary CTA:      After supporting content
3. Rule:               One primary CTA per email. Maximum two CTAs total.
```

### CTA A/B Test Ideas

```
CTA ELEMENTS TO TEST
======================
| Element       | Variant A              | Variant B               |
|---------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Button text   | "Start free trial"     | "Try it free for 14 days"|
| Button color  | Brand primary color    | High-contrast color      |
| Surrounding   | No supporting text     | "No credit card needed"  |
| Placement     | Above fold only        | Above fold + after proof |
| Size          | Standard button        | Large button             |
| First person  | "Start your trial"     | "Start my trial"         |
```

## 4. Landing Page Copy

### Landing Page Structure

```
LANDING PAGE COPY STRUCTURE
=============================

1. HERO SECTION
   - Headline: Primary benefit (use headline formula)
   - Subheadline: Supporting detail or clarification
   - CTA: Primary action button
   - Visual: Screenshot, demo video, or hero image

2. SOCIAL PROOF BAR
   - Customer logos, "Trusted by X companies," or key metric

3. PROBLEM SECTION
   - 2-3 pain points your audience experiences
   - Use their language (pull from support tickets, reviews, interviews)

4. SOLUTION SECTION
   - How your product solves each pain point
   - Feature-benefit pairs (feature enables benefit)

5. FEATURES/BENEFITS (3-4 key ones)
   - Each feature: Icon + heading + 2-sentence description
   - Lead with the benefit, explain the feature second

6. SOCIAL PROOF (DETAILED)
   - Customer testimonial with name, role, company
   - Case study snippet with specific metric
   - Star rating or review score

7. HOW IT WORKS
   - 3-step process (simple, visual)
   - Step 1: [Action] — Step 2: [Action] — Step 3: [Outcome]

8. PRICING OR CTA SECTION
   - Pricing table (if applicable)
   - Or repeated CTA with benefit reinforcement

9. FAQ
   - Address top 5-7 objections
   - Keep answers concise (2-3 sentences each)

10. FINAL CTA
   - Restate the primary benefit
   - CTA button with supporting text (guarantee, free trial, etc.)
```

### Feature-Benefit Translation

```
FEATURE-BENEFIT CONVERSION TABLE
==================================
Never list features alone. Always translate to benefits.

| Feature                        | Benefit                                        |
|-------------------------------|------------------------------------------------|
| Multi-branch support          | Manage all locations from a single dashboard    |
| Automated tax calculations    | Never miscalculate tax again                    |
| Real-time inventory tracking  | Know exactly what is in stock, right now         |
| Role-based access control     | Everyone sees only what they need                |
| Automated approval workflows  | Purchases get approved without chasing managers  |
| Audit trail on every record   | Full accountability for every change             |
| Customizable document numbers | Your invoices, your format, your brand           |
| Batch/serial tracking         | Trace any product back to its source instantly    |

FORMULA: [Product] has [feature] so you can [benefit],
         which means [outcome the reader cares about].
```

### Objection Handling in Copy

```
COMMON OBJECTION PATTERNS
===========================

OBJECTION: "It is too expensive."
RESPONSE PATTERN: Reframe the cost as an investment.
  "For less than the cost of one accounting error, you get
   a system that prevents all of them."

OBJECTION: "We do not have time to switch."
RESPONSE PATTERN: Address the migration concern directly.
  "Most teams are fully running within 2 weeks. We handle
   data migration. Your existing processes keep running
   until you are ready to switch."

OBJECTION: "We are too small / too big for this."
RESPONSE PATTERN: Show range with proof.
  "Teams of 5 use it. Teams of 500 use it. The system
   scales with you."

OBJECTION: "What if it does not work for us?"
RESPONSE PATTERN: Risk reversal.
  "Try it free for 30 days. If it does not save you time,
   cancel with one click. No contracts. No hassle."

OBJECTION: "Our current system works fine."
RESPONSE PATTERN: Quantify the hidden cost of the status quo.
  "Your team spends 15 hours a month on manual reconciliation.
   That is 180 hours a year. What would you do with that time back?"
```

## 5. Email Copy

### Subject Line Formulas

```
EMAIL SUBJECT LINE PATTERNS
=============================

CURIOSITY:
  "The one thing your [competitors/peers] are doing differently"
  "We looked at your [account/data]. Here is what we found."

BENEFIT:
  "Cut your [process] time in half"
  "[Outcome] in [timeframe] — here is how"

PERSONALIZATION:
  "[First name], your [month] report is ready"
  "A quick question about [company name]"

URGENCY (only when real):
  "[Offer] expires [day]"
  "Last chance: [specific thing]"

SOCIAL PROOF:
  "How [customer name] [achieved result]"
  "Join [number] teams already using [product]"

QUESTION:
  "Still managing [task] in spreadsheets?"
  "What would you do with 10 extra hours a week?"

RULES:
- 30-50 characters for optimal open rates
- Front-load the key word (mobile truncation)
- No ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation
- Preview text should complement, not repeat, the subject
- Test 2-3 variants per campaign
```

### Email Body Copy Structure

```
EMAIL BODY TEMPLATE
=====================

OPENING (1-2 sentences):
  - Hook: Reference a pain point, recent event, or shared context
  - Do NOT start with "I hope this email finds you well"

BODY (3-5 sentences):
  - Present the key message or offer
  - Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each)
  - Include one specific proof point (number, quote, result)

CTA (1 sentence):
  - Single, clear call to action
  - Button or link (not both)
  - Reiterate what they get when they click

SIGN-OFF:
  - Keep it simple
  - Include sender name and title for trust
```

**Email Example — Re-engagement:**

```
Subject: Your [Product] account misses you

Preview: Here is what changed since you last logged in

---

Hi [First name],

It has been 30 days since you last used [Product].
Since then, we shipped three features your team asked for:

1. Bulk invoice generation (process 100+ invoices in one click)
2. Custom report builder (build the exact report you need)
3. Slack integration (get notified without checking the app)

Want to see what is new? Log in and explore:

[CTA Button: "See what is new"]

If something was not working for you, reply to this email
and tell me. I read every response.

— [Name], [Title]
```

## 6. UX Writing and Microcopy

### Error Messages

```
ERROR MESSAGE FORMULA
======================
[What happened] + [Why] + [What to do next]

BAD:
  "Error 422"
  "Invalid input"
  "Something went wrong"

GOOD:
  "That email address is already registered. Try signing in instead."
  "The invoice date must be before the due date. Please update the dates."
  "We could not save your changes because the session expired.
   Please refresh the page and try again."

RULES:
- Never blame the user ("You entered an invalid..." -> "This field requires...")
- Be specific about what is wrong
- Tell them how to fix it
- Use plain language (no error codes without explanation)
```

### Empty States

```
EMPTY STATE COPY PATTERN
==========================
[What this area will contain] + [How to get started]

EXAMPLES:
  Invoices page (no invoices yet):
    "No invoices yet. Create your first invoice to start
     tracking payments and revenue.
     [Button: Create invoice]"

  Dashboard (new user):
    "Welcome to your dashboard. It will fill up with charts
     and metrics as you start using the system. Here is
     where to begin:
     1. Add your first customer
     2. Create a product
     3. Send your first invoice"

  Search results (no matches):
    "No results for '[query]'. Try a different search term
     or check the spelling. You can also browse [category]."
```

### Confirmation and Success Messages

```
CONFIRMATION MESSAGE PATTERNS
===============================

ACTION CONFIRMATION (before action):
  "Delete this invoice? This cannot be undone.
   [Cancel] [Delete invoice]"

  "Send this quote to john@company.com?
   [Cancel] [Send quote]"

SUCCESS MESSAGE (after action):
  "Invoice INV-2026-00042 created and sent to john@company.com."
  "Your changes have been saved."
  "Payment of $1,250.00 recorded against INV-2026-00042."

RULES:
- Confirm destructive actions (delete, send, submit)
- Show what was affected (specific record, name, amount)
- Offer an undo when possible
- Use the same language the user sees in the UI
```

### Button Labels and Form Copy

```
BUTTON LABEL GUIDELINES
=========================
- Use verb + noun: "Save draft," "Send invoice," "Add customer"
- Match the user's intent, not the system action
  Bad: "Execute" / Good: "Run report"
  Bad: "Process" / Good: "Record payment"
- Pair primary and secondary actions clearly:
  [Save and send] [Save as draft]
  [Confirm payment] [Cancel]

FORM LABEL GUIDELINES
=======================
- Labels above inputs (not placeholder text as labels)
- Help text below the field for complex inputs:
  "Tax ID" with help text: "Your 15-digit tax identification number"
- Required fields: Mark optional fields, not required ones
  (if most fields are required)
- Placeholder text: Show format examples
  "e.g., INV-2026-001" or "YYYY-MM-DD"
```

## 7. A/B Testing Copy

### What to Test

```
COPY ELEMENTS WORTH TESTING
==============================
| Element            | Test variants                              | Impact area     |
|--------------------|--------------------------------------------|-----------------|
| Headlines          | Benefit vs. feature, short vs. long        | Engagement       |
| Subject lines      | Curiosity vs. direct, personal vs. generic | Open rate        |
| CTA button text    | Action verb vs. benefit, 1st vs. 2nd person| Click rate       |
| Value proposition  | Different benefits emphasized               | Conversion rate  |
| Social proof       | Testimonial vs. stat vs. logo bar          | Trust/conversion |
| Form length        | 3 fields vs. 5 fields vs. 7 fields        | Form completion  |
| Pricing framing    | Monthly vs. annual, per-user vs. flat      | Purchase rate    |
| Objection handling | FAQ section vs. inline vs. none            | Conversion rate  |
```

### A/B Test Copy Framework

```
COPY TEST DOCUMENTATION
=========================
Test name:       [Descriptive name]
Hypothesis:      Changing [element] from [A] to [B] will [expected outcome]
                 because [reasoning].
Element tested:  [Headline / CTA / Subject line / etc.]
Variant A:       [Control — current copy]
Variant B:       [Test — new copy]
Primary metric:  [Click rate / Conversion rate / Open rate]
Sample size:     [Minimum required for significance]
Duration:        [How long the test will run]
Result:          [Winner, confidence %, lift %]
Learning:        [What this tells us about our audience]

EXAMPLE:
Test name:       Homepage headline — benefit vs. question
Hypothesis:      A question headline will increase demo requests by 10%
                 because it engages the reader's curiosity.
Variant A:       "Manage your entire business in one platform"
Variant B:       "What if managing your business took half the effort?"
Primary metric:  Demo request click rate
Result:          Variant B won (+14%, 96% confidence)
Learning:        Question-based headlines outperform statements for
                 our audience on the homepage.
```

```
TONE ADJUSTMENT GUIDE
=======================
| Channel          | Formality | Emotion   | Complexity | Example                                  |
|------------------|-----------|-----------|------------|------------------------------------------|
| Legal/Terms      | High      | Neutral   | Necessary  | "The Service Provider shall..."          |
| Product docs     | Medium    | Neutral   | Clear      | "To create an invoice, navigate to..."   |
| Blog post        | Medium    | Engaging  | Accessible | "Here is a smarter way to handle..."     |
| Landing page     | Medium    | Motivated | Simple     | "Get paid faster with automated..."      |
| Email campaign   | Low-Med   | Warm      | Simple     | "Hi Sarah, quick update on..."           |

```

### Writing for Different Audiences

```
AUDIENCE-SPECIFIC COPY ADAPTATION
====================================

TECHNICAL AUDIENCE (developers, IT admins):
  - Lead with specifics (API endpoints, configuration details)
  - Use technical terms without over-explaining
  - Show code examples
  - Skip the sales pitch — focus on capabilities and integration
  Example: "RESTful API with cursor-based pagination, Bearer auth,
           and JSON response envelopes. Rate-limited to 60 req/min."

BUSINESS AUDIENCE (managers, executives):
  - Lead with outcomes and ROI
  - Use plain language
  - Show metrics and results
  - Address risk and compliance
  Example: "Reduce month-end closing from 5 days to 2. Full audit
           trail for compliance. Real-time reports for board meetings."

END USERS (daily operators):
  - Lead with ease-of-use and time savings
  - Use task-oriented language
  - Show before/after comparisons
  - Address daily frustrations
  Example: "Create invoices in 3 clicks. No more copying from
           spreadsheets. The system fills in customer details,
           tax rates, and line items automatically."
```

## 9. Copy Formulas for Common Scenarios

### Pricing Page Copy

```
PRICING PAGE PATTERNS
======================
Plan names:   Use clear names (Starter, Professional, Enterprise)
              Avoid creative names that confuse (Galaxy, Nebula, Cosmos)

Plan descriptions (one sentence each):
  Starter:      "For small teams getting started with [core feature]."
  Professional: "For growing teams that need [key differentiator]."
  Enterprise:   "For organizations that require [advanced capability]."

Feature comparison:
  - List features in order of importance (most valuable first)
  - Use checkmarks, not feature names that need explanation
  - Highlight the recommended plan visually
  - Show annual savings percentage

FAQ section:
  - "Can I switch plans later?"
  - "Is there a free trial?"
  - "What happens when I exceed my plan limits?"
  - "Do you offer discounts for annual billing?"
  - "Can I cancel anytime?"
```

### Onboarding Copy

```
ONBOARDING SEQUENCE COPY
==========================

WELCOME (immediately after signup):
  Subject: "Welcome to [Product] — here is your first step"
  Body: One action only. The single most important thing to do first.
  CTA: "Complete your setup" or "Create your first [entity]"

DAY 1 (value delivery):
  Subject: "Create your first [thing] in 2 minutes"
  Body: Step-by-step walkthrough of core action.
  CTA: "Try it now"

DAY 3 (feature discovery):
  Subject: "Did you know you can [useful feature]?"
  Body: Introduce a feature that complements what they did on day 1.
  CTA: "Explore [feature]"

DAY 7 (social proof):
  Subject: "How [customer] uses [Product] to [outcome]"
  Body: Brief case study relevant to their use case.
  CTA: "See the full story" or "Try this approach"

DAY 14 (check-in):
  Subject: "How is [Product] working for you?"
  Body: Ask for feedback. Offer help.
  CTA: "Reply to this email" or "Book a call with our team"
```

## 10. Copy Review Checklist

```
COPY REVIEW CHECKLIST
======================
Clarity:
- [ ] Can someone unfamiliar with the product understand this?
- [ ] Is every sentence necessary? (Cut ruthlessly)
- [ ] Are there any ambiguous words or phrases?
- [ ] Is the reading level appropriate for the audience?

Persuasion:
- [ ] Is there a clear value proposition?
- [ ] Are benefits stated, not just features?
- [ ] Is there social proof or evidence?
- [ ] Is there a single, clear CTA?

Voice:
- [ ] Does the tone match the channel and audience?
- [ ] Is it consistent with brand voice guidelines?
- [ ] Is active voice used throughout?
- [ ] Are sentences varied in length?

SEO (if applicable):
- [ ] Does the headline include the target keyword?
- [ ] Is the meta description compelling and keyword-rich?
- [ ] Are headings structured logically (H1 > H2 > H3)?

Accuracy:
- [ ] Are all claims factual and verifiable?
- [ ] Are statistics sourced?
- [ ] Are product descriptions accurate and current?
- [ ] Are pricing and offer details correct?

Accessibility:
- [ ] Is the language inclusive?
- [ ] Are acronyms defined on first use?
- [ ] Is the copy scannable (short paragraphs, bullets, headings)?
- [ ] Do images have alt text that conveys meaning?
```
